## HIGH-IMPACT DISCREPANCIES (Would Change Skill Design)

### DISCREPANCY-01: C-PP-04 — COCOMO II "5x cost multiplier" may be an overclaim

**Claim:** "A component with Low SU multiplies adaptation cost by 5x even for
trivial changes." **Source evidence:** D3a says SU factor applies a 10-50%
penalty on three criteria. The 5x figure is not directly in D3a. It may derive
from combining multiple COCOMO II adjustments. **Impact:** If the real figure is
a 50% cost increase (not 500%), the urgency of portability scoring diminishes.
The insight remains valid (poor documentation drives up adaptation cost
significantly), but the magnitude is uncertain. **Recommendation:** State as
"documented significant cost multiplier (up to 50% penalty per COCOMO II SU
factor)" not "5x."

### DISCREPANCY-02: C-PP-06 — Portability rubric confidence overstated in RESEARCH_OUTPUT

**Claim (RESEARCH_OUTPUT):** Listed as HIGH confidence. **Source evidence:** D3a
explicitly says "No unified empirical study validates the 5-dimension composite
portability rubric against real-world adoption outcomes." The claims.jsonl
correctly assigns MEDIUM. **Impact:** If the rubric's threshold scores (>=10,
6-9, <6) are presented as validated gates in the skill, teams may over-rely on
them. They are hypotheses to test. **Recommendation:** Skill should present
rubric as "proposed framework, log scores + outcomes to build empirical
validation dataset over time."

### DISCREPANCY-03: C-RD-16 — Phind vs Perplexity confidence overstated in RESEARCH_OUTPUT

**Claim (RESEARCH_OUTPUT):** Listed as HIGH confidence. **Source evidence:** D2d
Finding 6 explicitly states this is from "community blogs" — single third-party
comparison, not independently validated. Claims.jsonl correctly assigns MEDIUM.
**Impact:** Low design impact — this is a discovery tool preference, not an
architectural decision. But the RESEARCH_OUTPUT Section 3.4 should not present
the 95%/80% figures as if they were rigorous benchmarks.

### DISCREPANCY-04: C-AI-13 — Agent Skills specification attribution discrepancy

**Claim:** "Anthropic released the Agent Skills specification as an open
standard in December 2025; OpenAI adopted the same format for Codex CLI."
**Source evidence:** D4c Findings states MCP was "donated to the Agentic AI
Foundation under the Linux Foundation" in December 2025. The "Agent Skills
specification" is a separate concept from MCP. D4c does not document OpenAI
adopting an Anthropic-originated "Agent Skills specification" — it documents
AGENTS.md (a different cross-tool standard) emerging from a collaboration
including Sourcegraph, OpenAI, Google, Cursor, and others in mid-2025. The claim
conflates two distinct developments: MCP standardization and AGENTS.md
emergence. **Impact:** [DESIGN-IMPACT: LOW for skill design, but MEDIUM for
ecosystem framing] The claim as stated is misleading — it suggests
Anthropic-to-OpenAI adoption of a single spec, when the reality is a broader
multi-organization standard (AGENTS.md) plus the separate MCP donation. The
ecosystem convergence point is correct; the attribution chain is wrong.
**Recommendation:** Correct to: "Anthropic donated MCP to the Agentic AI
Foundation (Linux Foundation) in December 2025. Separately, AGENTS.md emerged as
a cross-tool portability standard supported by Anthropic, OpenAI, Google,
Sourcegraph, Cursor, and others."

### DISCREPANCY-05: C-AI-08 — "17-point retrieval accuracy decline" source chain is soft

**Claim:** "A 17-point retrieval accuracy decline (93% to 76%) was measured as
context fills on Claude Opus 4.6 at 1M tokens." **Source evidence:** D4b Finding
9 states: "A 17-point retrieval accuracy decline (93% to 76%) was measured as
context fills on Claude Opus 4.6 at 1M tokens" and cites "Anthropic's own
context engineering research." D4b sourceIds include D4b-S5
(anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering) and D4b-S21
(vadim.blog), D4b-S22 (factory.ai). The finding is sourced to secondary analysis
of Anthropic research, not a directly linked official Anthropic publication with
these exact figures. **Impact:** [DESIGN-IMPACT: MEDIUM] The core insight
(context degrades with fill) is well-supported by multiple sources. The specific
93%→76% figures may be secondary-source aggregation. Do not use the exact
numbers in skill documentation; use the directional finding.
