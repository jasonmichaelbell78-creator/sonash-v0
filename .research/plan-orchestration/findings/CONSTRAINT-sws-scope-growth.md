# User Constraint: SWS Scope Growth

**Added:** 2026-03-24 (mid-research, after Wave 3) **Source:** User
clarification

## Constraint

SWS is a **plan for making plans**. Its scope will increase as it progresses
because each ecosystem standardization phase discovers new work that needs
planning and execution.

## Implications for Scheduling

1. The 80-130 session estimate from S-07 is a **floor, not a ceiling**
2. SWS chunks (from S-16) will likely spawn sub-plans during execution
3. Interleaving other work between SWS chunks is even MORE valuable because SWS
   pauses let you absorb newly discovered scope
4. The dependency graph (S-09) may gain new edges as SWS discovers interactions
   with other plans
5. Risk analysis (S-17) should treat SWS scope growth as a top risk factor

## Impact on Research Conclusions

- Scheduling algorithms (S-14) should account for variable-scope tasks
- The "SWS dominates critical path" finding becomes even stronger
- Parallel execution of non-SWS plans becomes more urgent — get them done before
  SWS scope inflation makes the overall timeline longer
