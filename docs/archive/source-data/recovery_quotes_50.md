# 50 Recovery Quotes (12-Step–Heavy)

A curated list of widely used recovery quotes and slogans. **Author/source is
included when clearly known**; otherwise marked **Anonymous / fellowship
saying**.

---

## Alcoholics Anonymous / Al‑Anon / 12‑Step sayings (1–25)

1. “Our common welfare should come first; personal recovery depends upon A.A.
   unity.” — _Alcoholics Anonymous, Twelve Traditions (Tradition One)_
2. “Rarely have we seen a person fail who has thoroughly followed our path.” —
   _Alcoholics Anonymous (Big Book), Ch. 5 “How It Works”_
3. “Half measures availed us nothing.” — _Big Book, Ch. 5 “How It Works”_
4. “Those who do not recover are people who cannot or will not completely give
   themselves to this simple program…” — _Big Book, Ch. 5 “How It Works”_
5. “If you have decided you want what we have and are willing to go to any
   length to get it—then you are ready to take certain steps.” — _Big Book, Ch.
   5 “How It Works”_
6. “God could and would if He were sought.” — _Big Book, Ch. 5 “How It Works”_
7. “We claim spiritual progress rather than spiritual perfection.” — _Big Book,
   Ch. 5 “How It Works”_
8. “We alcoholics are men and women who have lost the ability to control our
   drinking.” — _Big Book, Ch. 3 “More About Alcoholism”_
9. “We are without defense against the first drink.” — _Big Book, Ch. 3 “More
   About Alcoholism”_
10. “Practical experience shows that nothing will so much insure immunity from
    drinking as intensive work with other alcoholics.” — _Big Book, Ch. 7
    “Working With Others”_
11. “We will not regret the past nor wish to shut the door on it.” — _Big Book,
    Ch. 6 “Into Action” (often called part of the “Promises”)_
12. “Sometimes quickly, sometimes slowly.” — _Big Book, Ch. 6 “Into Action”_
13. “It works—it really does.” — _Big Book, Ch. 6 “Into Action”_
14. “We are not a glum lot.” — _Big Book, Ch. 9 “The Family Afterward”_
15. “A great turning point in our lives came when we sought for humility as
    something we really wanted…” — _Twelve Steps & Twelve Traditions (12&12),
    Step Seven_
16. “One Day at a Time.” — _Al‑Anon / fellowship slogan_
17. “Live and Let Live.” — _Al‑Anon / fellowship slogan_
18. “Let It Begin with Me.” — _Al‑Anon declaration / fellowship saying_
19. “Let Go and Let God.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
20. “Easy Does It.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
21. “First Things First.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
22. “Keep It Simple.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
23. “Think.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
24. “Progress, Not Perfection.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
25. “Just for today…” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_

## Narcotics Anonymous / NA sayings (26–35)

26. “We do recover.” — _Narcotics Anonymous, Basic Text (“We Do Recover”)_
27. “Lost dreams awaken and new possibilities arise.” — _NA Basic Text (“We Do
    Recover”)_
28. “There is no model of the recovering addict.” — _NA Basic Text (“We Do
    Recover”)_
29. “When the drugs go and the addict works the program, wonderful things
    happen.” — _NA Basic Text (“We Do Recover”)_
30. “We can lose the desire to use.” — _NA Basic Text (commonly quoted recovery
    theme)_
31. “We have found a way out…” — _NA Basic Text (commonly quoted recovery
    theme)_
32. “We learned that we must live without drugs in order to live.” — _NA Basic
    Text (commonly quoted recovery theme)_
33. “Just for today my thoughts will be on my recovery…” — _NA “Just for Today”
    framing (widely used in NA culture)_
34. “Keep coming back.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_
35. “It works if you work it.” — _Anonymous / fellowship saying_

## Widely used recovery quotes from named authors (36–50)

36. “God, give us grace to accept with serenity the things that cannot be
    changed…” — **Reinhold Niebuhr** (Serenity Prayer; commonly used in
    AA/Al‑Anon)
37. “Spiritus contra spiritum.” (“spirit against spirit”) — **Carl Jung** (often
    referenced in recovery history)
38. “The opposite of addiction is not sobriety. The opposite of addiction is
    connection.” — **Johann Hari**
39. “The mentality and behavior of drug addicts and alcoholics is wholly
    irrational…” — **Russell Brand**
40. “And so rock bottom became the solid foundation on which I rebuilt my life.”
    — **J.K. Rowling**
41. “Recovery is something that you have to work on every single day… it doesn’t
    get a day off.” — **Demi Lovato**
42. “Nobody stays recovered unless the life they have created is more rewarding
    and satisfying than the one they left behind.” — **Anne Fletcher**
43. “The chains of habit are too weak to be felt until they are too strong to be
    broken.” — commonly attributed to **Samuel Johnson** (attribution debated)
44. “I can be changed by what happens to me. But I refuse to be reduced by it.”
    — **Maya Angelou**
45. “Our greatest glory is not in never falling, but in rising every time we
    fall.” — commonly attributed to **Confucius** (attribution debated)
46. “Between stimulus and response there is a space…” — commonly attributed to
    **Viktor Frankl** (attribution debated)
47. “The greatest discovery of my generation is that human beings can alter
    their lives by altering their attitudes of mind.” — commonly attributed to
    **William James** (attribution debated)
48. “Remember just because you hit bottom doesn’t mean you have to stay there.”
    — commonly attributed to **Robert Downey Jr.** (attribution debated)
49. “Sobriety delivers what alcohol promises.” — _Anonymous / recovery saying_
50. “You can’t think your way into right acting; you can act your way into right
    thinking.” — _Anonymous / recovery saying_
