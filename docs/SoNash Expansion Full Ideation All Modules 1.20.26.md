# SoNash Expansion: Module 1 – Step Work Depth

**Focus:** Deepening engagement through structured, principle-based workflows.**Tech Stack Approach:** "Vibe Coder" (Start as text/forms -> Upgrade to interactive UI).

* * *

## Step 1: Honesty – The Admission

**Principle:** *Honesty* | **Focus:** *Admitting powerlessness & unmanageability.*

### 1. Principle Task: "Defining Honesty" Reflection

* **The Task:** A simple prompt asking: *"What is the difference between 'telling the truth' and 'being honest'?"*
* **Interaction:** A split screen showing "Old Definition" (e.g., Not getting caught) vs. "New Definition" (e.g., Being real with myself).
* **Why:** Re-frames honesty as an internal act of courage, not just an external legal confession.

### 2. The "Unmanageability Map" (Interactive Canvas)

* **The Tool:** A visual interface where users map addiction's impact on 4 areas: **Body**, **Mind**, **Relationships**, **Wallet**.
* **Action:** Users tap an area to add specific tags (e.g., "Insomnia" under Body, "Debt" under Wallet).
* **Output:** A visual "heat map" showing exactly where life has become unmanageable.

### 3. The "Powerlessness" Checklist

* **The Tool:** A list of 20 common behaviors associated with loss of control (e.g., *Tried to stop and couldn't*, *Hid usage*, *Used when I didn't want to*).
* **Action:** User checks off what applies. The visual accumulation of checkmarks acts as undeniable data to break denial.

### 4. Cost-Benefit Analysis (The Illusion of Control)

* **The Tool:** A 2-column comparison worksheet.
* **Column A:** "What using did FOR me" (Social ease, Numbing, Sleep).
* **Column B:** "What using cost me" (Trust, Self-respect, Money).
* **Action:** Seeing the "Cost" list physically longer than the "Benefit" list is a classic cognitive behavioral tool for acceptance.

* * *

## Step 2: Hope – The Belief

**Principle:** *Hope* | **Focus:** *Believing change is possible.*

### 1. Principle Task: The "Hope Meter"

* **The Task:** A slider bar (0-100%). Prompt: *"How much do you believe, right now, that things can get better?"*
* **Follow-up:** If they drag it to even 1%, the app celebrates: *"1% is all we need to start."*

### 2. The "Evidence of Recovery" Log

* **The Tool:** A "Collector" interface to gather external proof.
* **Action:** User logs 3 distinct data points:
  * *A person I know who stayed sober.*
  * *A story I heard that resonated.*
  * *A moment I survived without using.*
* **Why:** Builds a "case file" for hope when the user has none of their own.

### 3. "Defining Sanity" Worksheet

* **The Tool:** A re-framing exercise.
* **Action:** User compares two scenarios:
  * *My Old Reaction:* (e.g., Screaming, isolating, using).
  * *A Sane Reaction:* (e.g., Pausing, calling a friend, waiting).
* **Why:** Moves "sanity" from a medical term to a behavioral goal.

### 4. "Tiny Step": The Trust Experiment

* **The Tool:** A 1-hour challenge card.
* **Action:** *"For the next hour, I will act as if everything is going to be okay."* User taps "Start Timer" and journals one sentence when the hour is up.

* * *

## Step 3: Faith – The Decision

**Principle:** *Faith* | **Focus:** *Turning it over.*

### 1. Principle Task: "What is Faith?" Sorter

* **The Task:** A drag-and-drop game to de-stigmatize the concept.
* **Action:** User sorts cards into **"My Faith IS"** (e.g., Trust, Nature, Group, Love) vs. **"My Faith IS NOT"** (e.g., Punishment, Magic, Specific Religion).

### 2. The Decision & Values Contract

* **The Tool:** A formal digital document.
* **Action:** User selects 3 core values (e.g., Courage, Truth, Kindness).
* **The Signature:** User digitally signs: *"I decide to turn my will and life over to the care of these values/principles as I understand them."*

### 3. Control vs. Release Board

* **The Tool:** A Kanban-style board.
* **Action:** User types in current stressors (e.g., "My rent," "Mom's anger," "My sobriety"). They drag them into **"Can Control"** or **"Cannot Control."**
* **Why:** Visualizes the act of "turning it over."

### 4. "The Help Button" (Digital Surrender)

* **The Tool:** A giant, calm button.
* **Action:** Tapping it logs a timestamped entry: *"I am willing to accept help right now."* It’s a physical action for a spiritual decision.

* * *

## Step 4: Courage – The Inventory

**Principle:** *Courage* | **Focus:** *A fearless moral inventory.*

### 1. Principle Task: The "Fear vs. Courage" Scale

* **The Task:** A prompt acknowledging fear.
* **Prompt:** *"Courage isn't the absence of fear; it's acting despite it. What is one thing you are afraid to write down today?"*
* **Action:** User types it into a "Vanishing Box" (it disappears after typing to build trust).

### 2. The "Blueprint" Wizard (Guided Flow)

* **The Tool:** A step-by-step wizard to prevent overwhelm.
* **Action:** The app asks one question at a time: *"Who?"* → *"What happened?"* → *"How did it affect you?"* → *"What was your part?"*
* **Why:** Prevents the "blank page panic" of a massive spreadsheet.

### 3. Inventory Variants (The 4 Types)

* **The Tool:** Separate modules for **Resentments**, **Fears**, **Harms**, and **Sex/Relationships**.
* **Action:** Each module has specific prompts (e.g., Fear: *"What am I afraid of losing?"* vs. Resentment: *"How was my self-esteem hurt?"*).

### 4. The "Strengths" Inventory (The Balance)

* **The Tool:** A positive inventory list.
* **Action:** User lists 5 times they were brave, kind, or honest.
* **Why:** Reminds the user they are not "all bad"—this requires the courage to accept one's good traits too.

* * *

## Step 5: Integrity – The Confession

**Principle:** *Integrity* | **Focus:** *Admitting wrongs to self & others.*

### 1. Principle Task: "Secrets vs. Integrity" Check

* **The Task:** A reflection on the weight of secrets.
* **Prompt:** *"We are as sick as our secrets. How heavy does your backpack feel right now?"* (Visual slider: Light to Heavy).

### 2. The "Share Prep" Packet Generator

* **The Tool:** A document compiler.
* **Action:** Compiles Step 4 entries into a clean, readable PDF for the sponsor.
* **Feature:** **"Redaction Mode"**—User can blur out lines they absolutely aren't ready to say out loud yet (honoring their current limit).

### 3. The Listener Checklist

* **The Tool:** A vetting tool for safety.
* **Action:** Checklist for the person hearing the 5th Step: *Can they keep a secret? Are they non-judgmental? Do they understand addiction?*

### 4. Post-Share Decompression Log

* **The Tool:** A specific journal entry locked until *after* the share.
* **Prompt:** *"How do I feel right now?"* Captures the immediate feeling of lightness (integrity restored) to look back on later during hard times.

* * *

## Step 6: Willingness – The Readiness

**Principle:** *Willingness* | **Focus:** *Ready to have defects removed.*

### 1. Principle Task: The "Holding On" Analysis

* **The Task:** Exploring *why* we keep defects.
* **Prompt:** *"What are you afraid will happen if you let go of this behavior?"* (e.g., If I stop yelling, people will walk all over me).

### 2. The "Defect Extractor"

* **The Tool:** A data-pulling tool.
* **Action:** App pulls "My Part" entries from Step 4 and suggests common defects (e.g., "This sounds like Fear or Control").

### 3. "Ready or Not" Sliders

* **The Tool:** A willingness meter for specific defects.
* **Action:** User lists a defect (e.g., Gossiping). Slides bar from 0% ("I love doing this") to 100% ("Take it away now").
* **Why:** Honesty about *low* willingness is the key to this step.

### 4. The "Survival Value" Worksheet

* **The Tool:** A compassion exercise.
* **Action:** *"How did this defect help you survive in the past?"* (e.g., "Lying kept me safe from abuse").
* **Why:** Changing the narrative from "I am bad" to "I learned this to survive, but it no longer serves me."

* * *

## Step 7: Humility – The Asking

**Principle:** *Humility* | **Focus:** *Asking for removal.*

### 1. Principle Task: "True Humility" Definer

* **The Task:** Dispelling the "doormat" myth.
* **Action:** User selects definitions: *"Humility is thinking of myself less, not thinking less of myself."* vs *"Humility is being weak."*

### 2. Defect → Principle Swap

* **The Tool:** A mapping exercise.
* **Action:** User takes a defect (e.g., Fear) and maps it to the goal Principle (e.g., Faith).
* **Output:** A "Practice List" for the week.

### 3. The "Letting Go" Ritual (Interactive)

* **The Tool:** An animation or audio tool.
* **Action:** User writes a defect on a digital "paper." They swipe to "burn" it or record a short audio note asking for it to be removed.

### 4. The Humility Practice Log

* **The Tool:** Daily tracker.
* **Action:** *"Where did I practice the opposite of my defect today?"* (e.g., "I wanted to be right, but I chose to be kind").

* * *

## Step 8: Brotherly Love – The Willingness

**Principle:** *Brotherly Love* | **Focus:** *List of amends.*

### 1. Principle Task: "Love vs. Guilt" Check

* **The Task:** Motivation checker.
* **Prompt:** *"Am I doing this to clean up my side of the street (Love/Justice), or just to make them like me again (People Pleasing)?"*

### 2. Amends Candidate Sorter

* **The Tool:** List builder with filters.
* **Action:** User adds names. Sorts them into: **"Now"**, **"Later"**, **"Never/Unsafe"**.
* **Safety:** Pop-up warning if user flags someone as "Abusive."

### 3. Harm Analysis Card

* **The Tool:** Empathy builder.
* **Action:** For each name, answer: *"What did I do?"* and *"How did that likely make them feel?"* (This builds the "Brotherly Love" connection).

### 4. The Willingness Thermometer

* **The Tool:** A readiness gauge.
* **Action:** *"How willing am I to face this person?"* If low, prompt journaling about the barrier (Fear? Resentment?).

* * *

## Step 9: Justice & Love – The Action

**Principle:** *Justice & Love* | **Focus:** *Making amends.*

### 1. Principle Task: "Justice for All" Reflection

* **The Task:** Ensuring the amends doesn't create new harm.
* **Prompt:** *"Will making this amends cause more harm to them or others?"* (Ensures the Justice principle acts both ways).

### 2. The Amends Script Builder

* **The Tool:** Template generator.
* **Action:** Templates: *"I was wrong when I..."*, *"I am sorry for..."*, *"What can I do to make it right?"*
* **Why:** Keeps the script clean and prevents justifying/blaming.

### 3. Amends Tracker Board

* **The Tool:** Kanban board (To Do / Scheduled / Done).
* **Action:** Tracks status. Includes "Outcome Notes" (e.g., "They forgave me" or "Door slammed").

### 4. "Living Amends" Planner

* **The Tool:** Action plan for those we cannot contact.
* **Action:** *"Since I cannot speak to them, I will honor them by..."* (e.g., Being a present father today to make up for my absent father).

* * *

## Step 10: Perseverance & Self-Discipline – The Maintenance

**Principle:** *Perseverance/Self-Discipline* | **Focus:** *Continued inventory.*

### 1. Principle Task: "The Daily Discipline" Commitment

* **The Task:** A tiny habit contract.
* **Action:** *"I commit to pausing for 2 minutes today to check myself."*

### 2. The Nightly Spot-Check (Wizard)

* **The Tool:** The core daily retention loop.
* **Action:** 3 Questions: *1. Was I resentful/selfish/dishonest today? 2. Do I owe an apology? 3. What did I do right?*

### 3. The "Mid-Day Emergency" Button

* **The Tool:** Emotional regulation tool.
* **Action:** Tapping "I'm Spinning Out" triggers a spot inventory: *"Stop. Breathe. What is the real threat right now?"*

### 4. Conflict Resolution Wizard

* **The Tool:** Real-time processing.
* **Action:** For immediate fights: *"What just happened?"* → *"My Part"* → *"Do I need to clean this up now?"*

* * *

## Step 11: Spiritual Awareness – The Contact

**Principle:** *Spiritual Awareness* | **Focus:** *Prayer & Meditation.*

### 1. Principle Task: "The Noise vs. The Signal"

* **The Task:** A quick grounding exercise.
* **Action:** *"What is the loudest thought in your head right now? What is the quietest?"* (Tuning into awareness).

### 2. Morning Intention Setter

* **The Tool:** Dashboard widget.
* **Action:** *"Today I will practice [Patience]."*

### 3. Meditation Timer & Insight Log

* **The Tool:** Simple timer (1–20 min).
* **Action:** When timer ends, prompt: *"Did any guidance or clarity come to you?"*

### 4. Evening Review (Gratitude Mode)

* **The Tool:** Positive detection.
* **Action:** *"Where did I see my Higher Power/Recovery working in my life today?"*

* * *

## Step 12: Service – The Message

**Principle:** *Service* | **Focus:** *Carrying the message.*

### 1. Principle Task: "Service is Gratitude" Check

* **The Task:** Linking recovery to action.
* **Prompt:** *"You can't keep it unless you give it away. Who is one person you can check on today?"*

### 2. The Service Tracker

* **The Tool:** Action log.
* **Action:** Log small acts: *Called a newcomer, Made coffee, Listened to a friend.*
* **Metric:** "Service Points" (Internal metric, no leaderboard).

### 3. "Carry the Message" Log

* **The Tool:** Sponsee/Newcomer notebook.
* **Action:** Private notes on people you are helping (Names, Clean dates, Things to check in on).

### 4. Spiritual Awakening Journal

* **The Tool:** Long-term reflection.
* **Action:** Monthly Prompt: *"How is my reaction to life different today than it was a year ago?"*

# SoNash Expansion: Module 2 – Sponsor Tooling & Connection

**Core Philosophy:** "The Bridge."The app is a private sanctuary, but recovery requires connection. These tools bridge the gap between digital isolation and human accountability without creating a "surveillance state."

* * *

## Category 1: Sponsee Enacted (The Push)

*Tools the user initiates to share data, ask for help, or manage the relationship.*

### 1. Sponsor Export & "Redaction Pre-Flight"

*Merged: "Sponsor Export (PDF)" + "Redaction Pre-Flight Check"*

* **Expanded Description:** Sharing a journal is terrifying. This tool acts as a "security clearance" for the user's own thoughts. It generates a clean, printable PDF of specific worksheets or journals but gives the user absolute control *before* the file is generated.
* **The Workflow:**
  1. **Select Scope:** User chooses a specific Step (e.g., "Step 4") or date range.
  2. **Scan:** The app presents a scrollable, document-style preview of exactly what will be shared.
  3. **Redact:** User swipes across sensitive paragraphs to visually "black them out" or taps "Exclude" to remove sections entirely.
  4. **Annotate:** User adds a "Cover Note" (e.g., "I'm ready to talk about the blacked-out parts in person, just not in writing").
  5. **Send:** Generates the sanitized PDF to share via system native sheet (Signal, iMessage, etc.).

### 2. The "Hard Conversation" Script Library & Drafts

*Merged: "Ask Sponsor Draft" + "Script Library"*

* **Expanded Description:** When a sponsee is in trouble (relapse, resentment, fear), shame often causes silence. This tool lowers the friction of reaching out by providing pre-written, non-shaming templates for high-stakes moments.
* **The Tool:** A "Compose" screen with a "Template" button.
* **Template Examples:**
  * *The "I Slipped" Text:* "I need to be honest. I drank/used. I'm safe, but I need to talk when you're free."
  * *The "I'm Stuck" Text:* "I'm spinning my wheels on Step 4. Can we schedule 15 mins to unblock me?"
  * *The "Resentment" Text:* "I'm angry at you right now and I don't want to be. Can we talk about it?"
* **Action:** Tapping a template copies it to the system clipboard or opens the user's preferred messaging app, keeping the actual chat outside of SoNash for privacy.

### 3. "Mood-Stamped" Check-In & Quick Dial

*Merged: "Sponsee Help Request" + "Mood-Stamped Check-In"*

* **Expanded Description:** A text saying "I'm fine" is often a lie. This tool allows the user to communicate their emotional state instantly using visual data rather than paragraphs, paired with immediate access to support.
* **The Tool:** A "Check-In" generator.
* **The Workflow:**
  1. **Input:** User taps their current **HALT** status (Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired) and a **Willingness** slider (1–10).
  2. **Generate:** The app creates a small, clean image card (visualizing the data like a Spotify Share card).
  3. **Action:** The user can "Send Image" to their sponsor or tap the **"Quick Dial" FAB** (Floating Action Button) to call them immediately if the stats are in the danger zone.

### 4. Next Call Agenda ("The Parking Lot")

*Merged: "Next Call Agenda Builder" + "The Parking Lot"*

* **Expanded Description:** Sponsees often forget the critical issues they faced midweek by the time their scheduled sponsor call happens. This is a global "quick capture" system to ensure nothing important is lost.
* **The Tool:** A "Park for Sponsor" button available on every worksheet and journal entry.
* **The Workflow:**
  * *During the Week:* If a user hits a block or has a breakthrough, they tap "Park for Sponsor."
  * *During the Call:* The user opens the **"Agenda Mode."** All parked items are presented as a checklist (e.g., "Discuss fear from Tuesday," "Clarify Step 6 definition").
  * *After the Call:* User checks them off as "Discussed."

### 5. "Circle of Trust" & Invite Manager

*Merged: "Sponsor Invite Link" + "Circle of Trust"*

* **Expanded Description:** Recovery often involves more than one support person (Service Sponsor, Step Sponsor, Therapist). This permissions manager reflects that reality.
* **The Tool:** A contact management screen where the user assigns roles.
* **The Roles:**
  * *Primary Sponsor:* Access to shared Step Work & Safety Plans.
  * *Accountability Partner:* Access to Meeting Attendance & Daily Check-in stats only.
  * *Therapist:* Access to Mood Logs & specific journals only.
* **Action:** Generates unique, role-specific invite links that grant these specific read-only permissions (if the cloud sync feature is active) or sets defaults for manual exports.

### 6. The Sponsor Vetting Guide

*New Feature*

* **Expanded Description:** Newcomers often pick the wrong sponsor (predatory, unavailable, or incompatible) out of desperation. This private decision matrix helps them pause and evaluate safety.
* **The Tool:** A private checklist the user fills out *about* a potential sponsor.
* **The Checklist:**
  * *Do they have what I want?*
  * *Do they speak the language of recovery?*
  * *Do they respect boundaries?*
  * *Did they mention the Steps (or just their own opinions)?*
* **Outcome:**
  * *Mostly "Yes":* Offers the **"Script to Ask"** template.
  * *Mostly "No":* Displays a warning: "Take your time. You don't have to decide today."

* * *

## Category 2: Sponsor Enacted (The Pull/Collaborative)

*Tools designed to help the sponsor understand data, collaborate, or provide accountability. (Note: These are "Sponsor-Facing" but often generated/facilitated by the User's app).*

### 1. Sponsor Summary & "The Cheat Sheet"

*Merged: "Sponsor Summary Report" + "Sponsor's Cheat Sheet"*

* **Expanded Description:** Sponsors are often busy and don't know how to interpret raw app data. This feature turns a "Data Dump" into a "Training Manual" for the sponsor.
* **The Tool:** A one-page aggregate report with an educational footer.
* **The Report:** High-level stats (Days Sober, Meetings This Week, Mood Trend line) without private details.
* **The Cheat Sheet (Footer):** A static guide appended to the export:
  * *"How to read this: Look for patterns in the 'Time of Day' column on the mood log."*
  * *"Red Flags: If 'Resentment' counts are up, ask about their prayer/meditation routine."*
  * *"Safety Note: This user has flagged 'Trauma' on page 4—approach with care."*

### 2. The "Relapse Autopsy" Worksheet (Collaborative)

*New Feature*

* **Expanded Description:** After a relapse, the sponsor/sponsee relationship is often strained or chaotic. This tool provides a structured, neutral ground for debriefing, designed to be filled out *during* a call.
* **The Tool:** A specific worksheet locked until a "Reset" event occurs.
* **The Questions (Collaborative):**
  * *The Setup:* "What happened 24 hours *before* the event?" (e.g., skipped meal, argument).
  * *The Trigger:* "What was the exact moment the mental decision was made?"
  * *The Barrier:* "Why didn't I pick up the phone?" (Identifies the failure point).
  * *The Plan:* "What is ONE thing we change for next time?"
* **Output:** Generates a **"Safety Plan V2"** image card that saves to the user's home screen.

### 3. Shared Commitments ("The Digital Handshake")

*Merged: "Shared Commitments Board" + "Co-Sponsor Handshake"*

* **Expanded Description:** A mechanism for accountability that relies on integrity rather than gamified streaks. It digitally mimics looking someone in the eye and saying, "I did it."
* **The Tool:** A 2-way toggle board (requires linked accounts or manual "Witness" entry).
* **The Workflow:**
  1. **Agreement:** Sponsor and Sponsee agree on a goal (e.g., "90 meetings in 90 days" or "Call daily").
  2. **The Log:** Sponsee taps "Did it."
  3. **The Witness:** The Sponsor sees the update and taps "Witnessed" (a checkmark or 'fist bump' icon).
  4. **Offline Fallback:** If not digitally linked, the user manually checks a box: *"I reported this to [Sponsor Name] at [Time]."*

### 4. Sponsor Prompt Library

*Expansion of "Sponsor Prompt Library"*

* **Expanded Description:** Sometimes sponsors want to reach out but don't want to be overbearing. This resource provides them with gentle, effective questions to send to the user.
* **The Tool:** A web-viewable or exportable list of prompts the user can send to their sponsor: *"Here is a list of questions I respond well to."*
* **The Prompts:**
  * *"What is the loudest thought in your head right now?"*
  * *"Have you eaten something nutritious today?"*
  * *"Where did you see your Higher Power today?"*
  * *"Are you holding onto anything you need to let go of?"*

### 5. One-Time Share Code

*Expansion of "One-Time Share Code"*

* **Expanded Description:** A security feature for sponsors who do not have the app. It allows them to view a specific document securely without receiving a permanent file that could be leaked.
* **The Tool:** A link generator.
* **The Action:** User selects a document (e.g., "Step 4 - Fears"). App generates a URL.
* **The Security:**
  * **Time-Limited:** The link expires automatically in 24 hours.
  * **Read-Only:** The document cannot be edited.
  * **Revocable:** The user can tap "Kill Link" at any time to immediately revoke access.



# SoNash Expansion: Module 3 – Nashville Advantage (Local Utility)

**Core Philosophy:** "The Ground Truth."National apps have data; SoNash has *context*. These tools help users navigate the physical geography of Nashville while avoiding triggers and finding community.

* * *

## Part 1: Expansions of Existing Ideas

### 1. "Tonight in Nashville" (The Decision Engine)

* **The Problem:** A standard list of 50 meetings is overwhelming. An addict in decision-paralysis needs to be told where to go.
* **The Tool:** A time-sensitive, algorithm-lite suggestion engine.
* **Key Features:**
  * **The "Best Bet" Badge:** Highlights **one** meeting starting in the next 2 hours based on density/popularity (e.g., "The 7:00 PM at Club 12 is usually packed").
  * **The "Rush Hour" Filter:** Filters out meetings that are impossible to get to during Nashville traffic (4 PM - 6 PM) based on user location to prevent "road rage" triggers.
  * **"Get Me There" Button:** One-tap integration with Uber/Lyft/Maps that pre-fills the destination address.
  * **Offline Mode:** Downloads the next 12 hours of meeting data so it works even if service cuts out.

### 2. "Safe Spaces" Map (The Third Place)

* **The Problem:** "I have an hour to kill before the meeting, and I can't sit in a bar."
* **The Tool:** A map of curated "Third Places" (not home, not work, not a meeting).
* **Key Features:**
  * **"Late Night" Filter:** Crucial for Nashville. Highlights coffee shops or diners open past 9 PM (rare, but vital for sobriety).
  * **"Step Work Friendly" Tag:** Icons indicating spots that have big tables, cheap coffee, and low noise levels (e.g., specific libraries or quiet cafes).
  * **"NA Drink" Menu:** Highlights places that serve actual mocktails or decent coffee, not just soda gun water.
  * **Trigger Warning:** Notes if a "coffee shop" turns into a "wine bar" at 5 PM.

### 3. "My Sober Circuit" (Routine Builder)

* **The Problem:** Recovery relies on routine. Users need to reduce friction for their regular stops.
* **The Tool:** A "Favorites" system that acts like a personalized itinerary.
* **Key Features:**
  * **"Home Group" Pin:** The user pins their primary meeting. The app tracks "Consecutive Weeks Attended" (internal integrity metric).
  * **Routine Stacking:** User can build a "Tuesday Loop" (e.g., Gym -> 5:30 PM Meeting -> Diner).
  * **Traffic Alerts:** "Leave now to make the 7:00 PM." (Uses native OS travel time APIs).

* * *

## Part 2: New Ideas (Nashville Specific)

### 4. "The Meeting-After-The-Meeting" Guide

* **The Concept:** The meeting is where you learn; the parking lot or diner is where you heal. In Nashville, specific crowds migrate to specific spots after meetings.
* **The Tool:** A "Local Knowledge" tip section attached to major meetings.
* **Key Features:**
  * **The Tip:** "After the Tuesday night men's meeting, the guys usually go to [Specific Waffle House]."
  * **Community Sourced (Moderated):** Users can submit tips like "Great parking lot for talking," or "Don't rush off, they smoke outside for 30 mins."
  * **Why:** Helps the newcomer break into the social circle, which is the hardest part of early recovery.

### 5. The "Broadway Escape Plan" (Tourist/Trigger Mode)

* **The Concept:** Nashville is a party capital. If a user (local or tourist) gets stuck downtown (Broadway) surrounded by bachelorette parties and alcohol, they need an immediate "out."
* **The Tool:** A panic-navigation feature.
* **Key Features:**
  * **"Get Me Out" Button:** Instantly routes the user to the *nearest* quiet zone (e.g., the library downtown or a quiet hotel lobby) or the nearest meeting.
  * **"Safe Corridors":** Maps walking routes that avoid the densest bar streets.
  * **Why:** Preventing relapse in high-pressure environments.

### 6. The "Clubhouse Status" Hub

* **The Concept:** Nashville has key physical hubs (e.g., Club 12, 202 Friendship House, etc.) that operate differently than church-basement meetings.
* **The Tool:** A live dashboard for the major buildings.
* **Key Features:**
  * **"Open Now?":** Simple indicator if the building doors are unlocked (vital for homeless or struggling users).
  * **"Next 3 Meetings":** A marquee showing the upcoming schedule for *that specific building*.
  * **"Vending/Coffee":** Icons indicating if they have food/caffeine available on site.

### 7. "The First 72 Hours" (Nashville Edition)

* **The Concept:** People move to Nashville specifically for recovery. The first 3 days are chaotic.
* **The Tool:** A static, pre-loaded itinerary for a newcomer.
* **Key Features:**
  * **The Script:** "Day 1: Go to this assessment center (if needed). Go to this meeting (it's safe). Eat here (it's cheap)."
  * **The Transit Map:** A simplified overlay of the bus lines that hit the major recovery centers.
  * **"Where to wait":** Safe public places to sit while waiting for a bed or an appointment.

# SoNash Expansion: Module 5 – Journaling & Insights

# SoNash Expansion: Module 9 – Daily Engagement & Habits

**Core Philosophy:** "The Next Right Thing."We don't want the user glued to the phone. We want them to get in, get grounded, and get out into their life. These tools are designed to be "low friction, high impact" micro-interactions that build the muscle of recovery without the grind.

  * **What they SEE:** "Verified Actions" only. *Did they go to a meeting? Did they pass a drug test (if logged)? Are they at the Sober Living house (Geofence Check-in)?*
  * **What they DO NOT SEE:** Journal entries, Inventory, Chat logs.
  * **Why:** It automates "Proof of Life" so the spouse doesn't have to ask "Did you go?" 10 times a day.

### 8. "The 90-in-90 Passport" (Gamified Local Exploration)

* **The Concept:** Newcomers are told to do "90 meetings in 90 days." It's a grind. Let's make it an exploration of the city.
* **The Tool:** A digital "Stamp Book."
* **Feature:**
  * **The Challenge:** Visit 90 *unique* meetings in Nashville.
  * **The Verification:** GPS check-in or scanning a QR code at participating clubhouses.
  * **The Reward:** Unlocking local "perks" (e.g., a free coffee at a sober-friendly cafe) or just a rare digital badge.
  * **Why:** It pushes the user out of their comfort zone and forces them to meet new people across the entire city.

### 9. "The Service Exchange" (Gig Economy for Good)

* **The Concept:** Recovering addicts often have skills (mechanics, carpentry, coding) but no money. They need to help each other.
* **The Tool:** A barter-system bulletin board.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Post:** *"I am a mechanic. I will fix brakes in exchange for help moving furniture."*
    * **The Currency:** "Service Hours" (no money changes hands).
    * **The Safety:** Verified users only (Sober Living residents).
    * **Why:** It builds economic resilience and interdependence within the local recovery community.
  
  ### 10. "Voice from the Past" (Audio Time Capsule)
  
  * **The Concept:** The brain forgets the pain of addiction ("Euphoric Recall"). We need a message from the "Desperate Self" to the "Complacent Self."
  * **The Tool:** A locked audio vault.
  * **Feature:**
    * **The Recording:** On Day 1 (or during a crisis), the user records a video/audio: *"I am shaking. I lost my job. Please don't go back to this."*
    * **The Lock:** It is sealed.
    * **The Trigger:** If the user logs a "High Craving" or marks "Complacent" mood, the app asks: *"Do you want to listen to yourself from Day 1?"*
    * **Why:** It breaks the romanticization of the relapse.



# SoNash Expansion: Modules 12, 13, 14 (The Final Gaps)

**Overview:**These modules address the "Whole Person" (Finances, Body) and the "Long Tail" of recovery (Old Timers), ensuring the app remains valuable from Day 0 to Year 10.

* * *

## Module 12: Financial Repair ("The Sobriety Dividend")

### 1. "The Savings Ticker" (The Anti-Cost)

* **The Concept:** Users underestimate how much money they blew on their addiction. Seeing that money *accumulate* instead is a dopamine hit.
* **The Tool:** A cumulative calculator.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Input:** "I used to spend [$X] per [Day/Week]."
  * **The Ticker:** A live counter on the dashboard: *"You have saved $1,240 since getting sober."*
  * **The Purchase:** It translates that cash into rewards: *"That's equal to: 1 Month of Rent" or "A new Guitar."*

### 2. "The Wreckage List" (Debt Inventory)

* **The Concept:** Part of Step 9 (Amends) is often financial restitution. Avoiding creditors creates anxiety.
* **The Tool:** A debt snowball tracker (Safe/Private).
* **Feature:**
  * **The List:** User lists debts (Court Fines, Child Support, Personal Loans) without linking bank accounts (manual entry for privacy).
  * **The Plan:** User sets a tiny monthly goal (e.g., "$10/month").
  * **The Visual:** A progress bar for "Clearing the Wreckage."
  * **Why:** It turns a terrifying mountain of debt into a manageable game.

### 3. "The Envelope" (Budgeting for Beginners)

* **The Concept:** Newly sober people often don't know how to budget. Complex apps (Mint/YNAB) are overwhelming.
* **The Tool:** A "Cash Envelope" simulator.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Buckets:** "Rent," "Food," "Cigarettes/Vapes," "Recovery."
  * **The Logic:** Simple subtraction. *"You have $20 left for food this week."*
  * **Nashville Integration:** Links to local resources for financial aid or food banks if the "Food" bucket hits $0.

* * *

## Module 13: Bio-Rhythm & Wearables ("The Body Truth")

### 4. "The Stress Monitor" (Heart Rate Alerts)

* **The Concept:** High heart rate (HR) is a physical predictor of craving/agitation.
* **The Tool:** Integration with Apple Health / Google Fit.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Trigger:** If resting heart rate spikes (and user isn't moving), the app sends a silent notification.
  * **The Prompt:** *"Your heart is racing. Check in?"*
  * **The Action:** Deep-links to the **"Pause Protocol"** (Module 9) to breathe it down.

### 5. "Sleep Truth" (Recovery's Foundation)

* **The Concept:** Poor sleep is the #1 precursor to relapse. Users will claim they are "fine" while sleeping 3 hours a night.
* **The Tool:** Automated sleep correlation.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Data:** Pulls sleep duration from the phone/watch.
  * **The Insight:** *"You've slept <5 hours for 3 days. Your risk of irritability is high today."*
  * **The Nudge:** Suggests an earlier wind-down routine from Module 9.

### 6. "Movement as Medicine" (Step & Activity Tracker)

* **The Concept:** Depression keeps people on the couch. Motion changes emotion.
* **The Tool:** A non-fitness-obsessed activity log.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Goal:** "Move your body for 15 minutes." (Not "Burn 500 calories").
  * **The Nashville Twist:** *"Walk the Shelby Bottoms Greenway"* or *"Walk around Centennial Park"* suggestions.

* * *

## Module 14: The "Old Timer" Toolkit ("The Elder Mode")

### 7. "The Sponsee CRM" (Mentor Dashboard)

* **The Concept:** Sponsors manage multiple sponsees in their head. They forget anniversaries or who is on Step 4.
* **The Tool:** A private "Caseload" manager.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Profile:** "John D." (Clean Date: Jan 12). (Current Step: 4).
  * **The Notes:** *"Struggling with mom," "Call on Tuesdays."*
  * **The Reminder:** *"John's 1 year anniversary is next week. Send a text."*

### 8. "The Speaker's Outline" (Story Builder)

* **The Concept:** Old timers are asked to "share their story" at meetings. They often ramble or forget key points.
* **The Tool:** A story structuring tool.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Template:** "What it was like," "What happened," "What it's like now."
  * **The Timeline:** Pulls from their **"Scroll of Life"** (Module 11) to remind them of key turning points.
  * **The Output:** A simple cue-card view for holding while speaking at a podium.

### 9. "The Legacy Archive" (Wisdom Capture)

* **The Concept:** Long-term recovery generates wisdom. It shouldn't be lost.
* **The Tool:** A searchable database of their own insights.
* **Feature:**
  * **The Search:** User can search their 5 years of journals for "Grief" or "Job Loss" to see how they handled it last time.
  * **The Use Case:** When a sponsee asks about a problem, the sponsor can look up their own experience: *"Here is what I wrote when I went through that in 2024."*

## The Missing Glue: "Day 0" Onboarding

### 10. "The Intake Interview" (Chat-Based Setup)

* **The Concept:** Don't use a settings menu. Use a conversation.
* **The Tool:** A conversational wizard.
* **The Flow:**
  * *App:* "Hey. Welcome to SoNash. Tough day or good day?"
  * *User:* "Tough."
  * *App:* "Got it. Let's keep this simple. Do you want to find a meeting, or just vent?"
  * *User:* "Vent."
  * *App:* Opens the **Journal** immediately. No account creation, no setup. Just value.

### 11. "The Slow Rollout" (Progressive Disclosure)

* **The Concept:** Hide 80% of the app on Day 1.
* **The Feature:**
  * **Day 1:** Only Journal, Meeting Finder, and Panic Button are visible.
  * **Day 3:** Unlock "Check-Ins."
  * **Day 7:** Unlock "Step Work."
  * **Day 30:** Unlock "Service Tools."
* **Why:** Prevents cognitive overload.
